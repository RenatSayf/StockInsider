Part Two. Reporting.

In the first part, we learned that every legitimate insider after the transaction must report to the Securities and Exchange Commission (let me remind you, he oversees and regulation of the securities market). They do this according to a special form that it is called Form 4 SEC (or simply Form 4). This form is immediately after filling out published on the commission's website - www.sec.gov in the "FILINGS" section. Well, this is so for information, we will not read and process hundreds of different documents in search of the right of course, there are special sites for this, which do it for us for free, but more on that later. Let's first analyze Form 4, look at it below in the figure.

We have a small table in front of us, in which it is very conveniently done - the form itself is black, and the filled data is blue. So the form is well readable.

The structure of all completed forms is the same. So, let's go straight to decoding the form, and let's start with the title table. By points (according to the table):

  1. The name of the person who filled out the form, place of work and address. This is insider data, who submits the form
  2. Name the name of the company for which the insider transaction was made. As well as her ticker (this is a short name, the same ticker will be in your list of CFD instruments for stocks)
  3. The date of the earliest transaction. There may be several purchases or sales in the form, here the date of the very first transaction is indicated
  4. Correction to the form. It sometimes happens that the form is filled out with errors. Therefore, in order to to publish the correction of the error, you need to fill out form 4 again, and just in this the paragraph indicates the date of the first, erroneous form
  5. The relation of the person who fills out the application to the issuer. This is what we talked about in the first part, which insider. It can be the director of the company, some her other employee, the owner of 10% of the company's shares, or another person - in the stock below is detailed
  6. Type of application - one person, or a group of persons fills out the form

As we have seen, a fairly simple table. Now consider Table 1 - "Non-derivative securities" (Table I - Non-Derivative Securities Acquired, Disposed of, or Beneficially Owned), which is information important for us:

  1. The name of the type of security - here it will almost always be "common stock" - ordinary stocks.
  2. Transaction date 2A is an extended field 2, in principle, it does not matter for us.
  3. Transaction code. For us, only "P" - purchase, from the English word purchase, and "S" - sale of shares (from sale) are important. Other options are possible here, but they do not have for we have no weight.
  4. The number of shares, then the code (it is not important to us) and the price at which the operation took place.
  5. The number of shares owned by the person after the transaction. Here we can see, for example, if an insider sold the entire remaining package, the figure will be zero.
  6. The form of ownership of shares is direct or indirect. A straight line is when a person who filled out a form, personally owns shares, indirect - this is, for example, a person owns shares through some kind of fund, etc. Well, in general, it's also not particularly important, because an insider wants to make a profit regardless of whether he owns shares directly or indirectly.
  7. The nature of indirect ownership of shares, filled in exactly how the person owns the shares, who filled out the form. Also, not an essential field.

Table 2 - Derivative Securities (Table II - Derivative Securities Acquired, Disposed of, or Beneficially Owned), here we are talking about options, warrants, convertible securities. There are similar fields here, there is no need to stop at this table it doesn't make much sense, it's basically not used for trading.

And even below there is an item "Explanation of Responses" (notes) - here there is an explanation to footnotes of the completed form. This point sometimes has weight, but I haven't considered it for a long time it, and this does not prevent you from earning, in principle, you can also miss it.

Below, as in all documents, the insider's signature and the date of filling in. That's the whole form. Nothing complicated, but a lot of useful.
